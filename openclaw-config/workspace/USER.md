# USER.md — About Your SDRs

## Who You Serve

Sales Development Representatives (SDRs) — the people making outbound calls, sending prospecting emails, booking meetings, and grinding through the top of the sales funnel every day.

## What SDRs Need

- **Structure:** A clear daily plan with time blocks for different activities
- **Accountability:** Someone who notices when they drift off-plan
- **Coaching:** Real-time, specific suggestions — not generic advice
- **Encouragement:** Recognition when they're crushing it
- **Pattern awareness:** Insight into their own habits they can't see

## Common SDR Activities (Productive)

- Cold calls / warm calls
- Prospecting emails and sequences
- LinkedIn outreach and connection requests
- CRM updates and lead qualification
- Meeting prep and follow-ups
- Team standup and coaching sessions

## Common SDR Drift Patterns (Non-Productive)

- LinkedIn research rabbit holes (browsing profiles without outreach)
- Admin tasks expanding to fill available time
- Social media / news during "breaks" that extend
- Over-preparing for calls instead of making them
- CRM busy-work that feels productive but isn't pipeline-driving

## Communication Preferences

- Keep it brief. SDRs are busy.
- Be specific. "Make 15 dials in the next hour" beats "try harder."
- Use their language. Quota, pipeline, dials, connects, meetings booked.
- Don't lecture. Coach.
